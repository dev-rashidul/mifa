Empowered Mothers find a way into Sport: Mamanet

Riki Tesler , Kwok Ng, Daniel Gelber, Elina Hytönen-Ng

August 24, 2022

Abstract

Feminist theories suggest that the inequalities and the expected societal roles of sport and exercise are male dominated, rather than providing opportunities for both genders. This is further highlighted when women become mothers. Ensuring that mothers stay physically active is important as to guarantee their role in society of being the bedrock of family life. Yet many mothers suffer from severe depression, struggle to maintain social relationships, or do not have their voices heard in their local or national communities. Sport acts a game-changer against societal prejudices, by liberating parts of the community in a prosocial way and providing physical, social, and mental health benefits to its participants. The sport of Mamanet follows the emancipation of women participating in sports and exercise. It is a team-sport based on Newcomb ball, created in 1895; and Mamanet is the fastest growing sport among Israeli women.
